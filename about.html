About the study

The PROPER 2 calculator has been developed by the PROPER study group through prospective clinical trials on prostate salvage radiotherapy (SRT) in patients with biochemical recurrence after prostatectomy.

Results from these studies demonstrate that the PSA response during SRT is strongly associated with the risk of a new prostate cancer recurrence. We have quantified the PSA response by a PSA decay-rate constant (kPSA) using first order kinetics, i.e. -ln(PSA) ~ kPSA x time (1).

We have shown that kPSA together with prognostic clinical factors can predict treatment outcome with high accuracy.

The calculator predicts the risk (P) of tumour recurrence within three years after SRT. It is determined with a logistic regression model including kPSA (based on PSA response during the first four weeks of SRT), PSA at start of SRT, Gleason score, surgical resection margins and time between prostatectomy and SRT.

This calculator is used in the prospective randomized phase III trial called PROPER 2 for estimating the risk of a new prostate cancer recurrence within three years in order to select patients for randomization between receiving an addition of pelvic lymph node irradiation or not.
